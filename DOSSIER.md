## Behavioural Profile

### Strengths:
- High training consistency
- Strong aerobic durability
- Good engagement with data and analysis tools (WKO5, TrainingPeaks)

### Risks / Limiters:
- Tendency to overtrain / add extra load beyond plan
- Fatigue signals often ignored or misinterpreted

### Key fatigue indicators:
- Inability to elevate heart rate during efforts
- Loss of top-end power / “no kick”
- Reduced responsiveness in high-intensity sessions

---

## Coaching Requirements

- Maintain structured framework with defined weekly intent
- Allow controlled flexibility around group rides
- Avoid stacking high-intensity days back-to-back
- Emphasise:
  - VO2max progression (short → long intervals)
  - Threshold durability
  - Recovery discipline

### Decision rules:
- Do NOT prescribe recovery purely based on TSB unless corroborated by:
  - HR suppression
  - Subjective fatigue
  - Performance drop
- Prioritise quality of key sessions (Tue, Wed, Fri)
